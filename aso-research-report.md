## 5. ASO BEST PRACTICES 2025-2026 — What Works NOW

### Algorithm Changes You MUST Know

#### iOS App Store (June 2025 Update)
1. **Screenshot caption text is now indexed.** Apple uses OCR to read text in screenshot captions and factors it into keyword rankings. Apps with caption keywords saw **up to 22% visibility boost within 30 days.**
2. **Semantic matching replaces exact matching.** Apple now understands intent — "learn hangul" may match "korean alphabet" searches.
3. **Custom Product Pages doubled from 35 to 70.** Each CPP can have manually assigned keywords for search results.
4. **Search intent diversity** — Apple now shows varied app types in results rather than only one category.

#### Google Play (2025-2026)
1. **Health metrics penalize low-quality apps.** Crash rates, battery usage, and ANR rates affect ranking.
2. **Starting March 1, 2026, non-compliant apps excluded from discovery surfaces.**
3. **Guided search** introduced September 2025 — Google shows intent refinement options.
4. **Full description is fully indexed.** Every keyword in your 4,000-char description counts.
5. **Reviews are fully indexed.** If users mention "kpop" or "kdrama" in reviews, you rank for those terms.

### Metadata Best Practices
- **Title keywords carry strongest weight** on both platforms
- **iOS Subtitle** is the second-strongest signal after the title
- **iOS Keywords field:** 100 chars, comma-separated, no spaces, no duplicates. Each word counted only once even if repeated
- **Update metadata every 3-4 weeks** to test and iterate. Separate major changes to measure impact
- **Don't repeat keywords** across title, subtitle, and keywords field on iOS (waste of characters — Apple indexes each unique word once)

### The Cross-Localization Hack (iOS Only)
The US App Store indexes **9 secondary languages**: English (US), Spanish (MX), Arabic, Chinese (Simplified & Traditional), French, Korean, Portuguese (Brazil), Russian, Vietnamese.

**Each secondary locale gives you an additional 160 characters of indexable metadata** (30 title + 30 subtitle + 100 keywords).

**For koko AI specifically:**
- Fill in Spanish (MX) locale with Spanish-language keywords: "aprender coreano", "kpop coreano", "idioma coreano"
- Fill in Korean locale with Korean keywords: "한국어 배우기", "케이팝", "한국어 공부"
- Fill in Portuguese (Brazil): "aprender coreano", "kpop coreano", "drama coreano"
- Fill in French: "apprendre le coréen", "kpop coréen"
- **This can expand your keyword pool from 160 to 800+ indexable characters in the US alone**

## 8. RATING & REVIEW STRATEGY

### Current Benchmarks
- Apps rated **below 3.5 stars** rank for 3x fewer keywords
- Apps at **4.4+ stars** see ~25% more downloads
- **79% of users** check ratings before downloading
- Conversion rate drops dramatically below 4.0 stars

### Systematic 5-Star Review Strategy

#### 1. Smart In-App Rating Prompt Timing
**When to prompt (high-satisfaction moments):**
- After completing their first lesson successfully
- After learning their first K-pop song vocabulary set
- After achieving a 3-day or 7-day streak
- After receiving a positive AI tutor response ("Great pronunciation!")
- After unlocking a new K-drama vocabulary pack

**When NOT to prompt:**
- During onboarding or first launch
- After a crash or error
- After a failed quiz
- During payment/subscription flows
- More than 3 times per year (Apple limits SKStoreReviewController to 3 prompts/year)

#### 2. Pre-Review Sentiment Filter
```
Step 1: Show internal "How are you enjoying koko?" prompt
Step 2: If positive → Route to App Store / Google Play review
Step 3: If negative → Route to in-app feedback form / support
```
This prevents negative reviews from reaching the store.

#### 3. Review Keyword Seeding (Google Play Only)
Since Google Play indexes review text, encourage specific language in review prompts:
- "Tell others what you love about learning Korean with koko!"
- "How has koko helped you understand K-pop or K-drama?"
- Users naturally mention keywords like "Korean", "kpop", "kdrama", "learn" — boosting your keyword rankings.

#### 4. Review Response Strategy
- **Respond to ALL 1-2 star reviews within 48 hours** (most likely to update if they see a genuine response)
- **Focus on reviews from the last 90 days** — these are most likely to be updated
- Use empathetic, solution-oriented language
- Apple's AI-generated review summaries (iOS 18.4+) will highlight patterns — fix recurring complaints FAST

#### 5. Review Velocity Targets
- **Month 1-3:** Target 50+ reviews (quality over quantity)
- **Month 4-6:** Target 200+ reviews
- **Month 6-12:** Target 1,000+ reviews
- Maintain **4.5+ star average** throughout

- Full English (US) optimization
- Cross-localize into all 9 US secondary locales (Spanish MX, Korean, Chinese, Portuguese BR, French, Arabic, Russian, Vietnamese)

#### Phase 2 — Japan (Week 3-4)
- Full Japanese localization: title, subtitle, keywords, description, screenshots
- Japan requires different creative preferences than US — more detailed information, pastel aesthetics
- TimeFlik saw **60% conversion improvement** after localizing for Japan

#### Phase 3 — Indonesia + Southeast Asia (Month 2)
- Bahasa Indonesia localization
- Thai localization
- Filipino/Tagalog localization
- Vietnamese (already have cross-localization data from US)

#### Phase 4 — Brazil + Latin America (Month 2-3)
- Portuguese (Brazil) full localization
- Spanish localization covers Mexico + all LATAM

#### Phase 5 — Europe (Month 3-4)
- French, German, Spanish (Spain)

### Critical Localization Tips
- **Don't just translate — adapt.** Keywords in Japanese are completely different search terms, not translations of English keywords
- **Localize screenshots.** Tiimo gained **70% more downloads in Japan** after localizing screenshots
- **Include emojis** in Japanese and Korean store listings — they increase conversion in those markets
- **Date/number formats** differ by market (Korea uses YYYY.MM.DD)
- **Cultural references** — mention locally popular K-pop groups (BTS is universal, but some groups are bigger in specific markets)
